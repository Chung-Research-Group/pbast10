Visa Information & Invitation Letter

PBAST10 welcomes participants from around the world. Entry requirements depend on nationality and may change before May 2027, so confirm your status through official Korean government sources.

Entry Requirements to the Republic of Korea

K-ETA — Electronic Travel Authorization

Some visa-free travelers may need K-ETA authorization. Temporary exemptions currently announced through 2026 may not apply to travel in 2027.

C-3 Visa — Short-Term Visit

Required for nationalities that are not visa-exempt. Apply at the Korean Embassy or Consulate in your country.

Processing times and documents vary by country. Check the Korea Visa Portal and your local Korean mission well in advance.

Official Invitation Letter

The PBAST10 Organizing Committee can issue an invitation letter to support a visa application after registration. K-ETA applications do not require a conference invitation letter. The request procedure will be announced.

  1. Complete your conference registration and payment
  2. Follow the invitation-letter request procedure when it is published
  3. Provide only the information requested for the letter through the designated secure channel
  4. Allow sufficient time for committee processing and your local visa procedure

Do not email passport scans or passport numbers unless the published procedure specifically requires them and provides a secure channel.

Important Notes

  • The invitation letter supports a visa application only; it does not guarantee approval or provide financial support
  • Visa and K-ETA decisions rest solely with Korean immigration authorities
  • Apply well in advance and follow the passport-validity rules that apply to your nationality and travel document
  • Use only official government websites and beware of third-party K-ETA application services
